Medical Terms?

FBS

Fasting blood sugar is a blood test that measures the amount of glucose (sugar) in your bloodstream after a period of fasting. It is commonly used to diagnose and monitor diabetes. FBS levels are typically taken after an overnight fast of at least 8 hours. Normal fasting blood sugar levels are generally between 70--99(mg/dL).

HbA1c

HbA1c is a blood test that reflects the average blood sugar level over the past 2-3 months. It measures the percentage of hemoglobin that is coated with sugar. HbA1c is used to assess long-term blood sugar control in individuals with diabetes. It provides a more stable picture of blood sugar levels. Normal HbA1c levels are from 4.2% to 5.7%
